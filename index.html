Take it slow and give your new cat time to adjust. Start by confining your cat to a small space with food, water, litter box, and a comfortable bed. Gradually introduce your cat to other rooms in the house and other pets or family members. Use positive reinforcement and plenty of treats to help your cat feel comfortable and safe.

Provide your cat with a healthy diet, plenty of fresh water, regular exercise, and lots of love and attention. Keep your cat's environment clean and safe, and provide toys and scratching posts to satisfy their natural instincts. Regular grooming and preventive care, such as vaccinations and parasite control, can also help keep your cat healthy and happy.

It's recommended to take your cat to the vet at least once a year for a checkup and preventive care, such as vaccinations and parasite control. If your cat has any health issues or is a senior, you may need to visit the vet more frequently.
